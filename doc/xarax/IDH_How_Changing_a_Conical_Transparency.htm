Changing conical transparency

A conical transparency applied to an object is a graduation of transparency from one level to another swept out in a radial fashion. The single semicircular transparency arrow used to control it has a handle on each end with an additional handle in the center. The handles on the ends of the arrow define the levels on either side of the conical transparency.
