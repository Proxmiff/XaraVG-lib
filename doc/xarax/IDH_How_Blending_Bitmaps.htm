Blending bitmaps

Using transparency and blending it is possible to create simple pseudo-morphing effects with Xara LX.

 To morph bitmaps

1. Convert both bitmaps to simple shapes (see Converting bitmaps to shapes).

2. Resize the bitmaps so they are the same size and shape (see Scaling objects).

3. Group the bitmaps (see Grouping objects).

4. Duplicate the group (CTRL+D.)

5. Move the duplicate group away from the original group (see Moving objects).

6. CTRL-click on the new group to select the top shape in it (see Selecting objects in objects).

7. Use the Transparency Tool to give it 100% transparency (see Applying flat transparency). You should now be able to see the lower shape.

8. Blend the two groups together (see Blending objects).

Tip

 If you only require one 'frame' from the sequence, apply Convert to Editable Shapes to the blend, ungroup and remove the required section. See Converting blends to shapes.
